Mental Illness Prediction in Middle School Students

Predicted mental health outcomes of middle school students. Using a dataset of over 500 middle school students, predicted anxiety and depression outcomes of these students. Students at a middle school in MA took the Revised Child Anxiety and Depression shortend 25 question questionaire (RCADS-25). This survey evaluates students at three different levels of depression and anxiety based on their responses (none/mild, moderate/borderline clinical and severe/clinical). Predicted which class of depression/anxiety students had based on their responses to the survey questions. From there used feature importances to determine which questions/symptoms were important for depression/anxiety. Perfomed analysis across gender and race.

2025 March Madness Bracket Simulator

Extracted advanced statistical data from college basketball teams in previous March Madness tournaments. Ran a random forest predictor to predict what round teams would make it to on the data. Used feature importances to see what stats were important for winning in the tournament. Built a bracket in Excel with current teams. Created a score function for each team using the feature importances from the random forest as weights for the different stats with a randmized multiplier attatched to the function. Team with the higher score in each matchup advanced to the next round until a champion was crowned. Ran 500 simulations in Excel.
